Marcus Aurelius · Meditations

"You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."

The situation isn't the problem. Your resistance to it is. Let go of what you can't control and the suffering disappears.

Seneca · Letters to Lucilius

"We suffer more in imagination than in reality."

Most of what keeps you up at night hasn't happened yet — and probably won't. The dread is self-made. The moment is survivable.

Epictetus · Enchiridion

"Men are disturbed not by things, but by the opinions about things."

Nothing has meaning until you assign it one. You can choose a different meaning. That's the whole game.

Rumi · The Masnavi

"The wound is the place where the light enters you."

Difficulty isn't a detour. It's the path. Stop waiting for it to end and start moving through it.

Viktor Frankl · Man's Search for Meaning

"When we are no longer able to change a situation, we are challenged to change ourselves."

Acceptance isn't defeat — it's the only door out. The fight against what already is costs everything and changes nothing.
